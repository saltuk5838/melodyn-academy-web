🤝 User Research & Collaboration

To analyze and evaluate user experience, we collaborated with the Beyaz Leke music group, who are professional musicians.

We asked them to use our platform as real users in order to:

Identify necessary features
Evaluate usability and user experience
Test ease of navigation and interaction

Additionally, we collected data through surveys and analyzed the results to improve our system.


🎯 Target Audience

Our target audience includes:

Individuals with limited budgets
People who do not have access to nearby music academies
Anyone who wants to learn to play a musical instrument


🧩 Task Analysis

The typical user journey is as follows:

The user wants to enroll in a course
The user logs into the system
The user completes a survey to determine their skill level
The user purchases a course
The user begins learning

🎨 Design Principles
Visibility:
The system status is clearly communicated at all times. Users can immediately understand which stage they are in and what each option represents.

Affordance:
Interface elements are designed to suggest their functionality. Shadows and depth effects are used to make buttons feel clickable and cards appear selectable.

Feedback:
Every user interaction is followed by immediate visual feedback. Users are informed through animations or visual changes when a selection is made or an action is completed.

Mapping:
A natural relationship is maintained between controls and outcomes. For example, selecting an instrument dynamically updates the course list.

Simplicity:
Unnecessary visual elements are avoided to keep the focus on the core content: music education.

Structure:
Information hierarchy is carefully organized from most important to least important, following a Z-layout for better readability and flow.

Consistency:
A consistent design language is maintained across all pages, including a unified color palette (Dark Theme + Golden Yellow) and typography.

Tolerance:
The system is designed to minimize user errors by including confirmation dialogs and easy navigation paths for reversing actions.

🧱 Interface Components
Navigation (Menu):
Used to enable quick transitions between pages such as Home, Survey, and Courses, while making the site hierarchy clearly visible.
Buttons:
Designed to be prominent and accessible in order to guide users toward critical actions such as starting the survey or registering.
Form (Instrument Survey):
Preferred to analyze user preferences and physical suitability through structured data. Multiple-choice questions are used instead of open-ended inputs to reduce errors and improve usability.
Modals (Pop-up Windows):
Integrated to present important notifications or quick results without disrupting the user’s position on the page.


🔄 UI Evaluation with Human-Action Cycle

User interactions on the platform are designed based on Don Norman’s Human Action Cycle:

Gulf of Execution:
When users enter the platform, they are presented with clear call-to-action elements such as the “Instrument Suitability Test”. The design minimizes the steps required to answer the question “Where should I start?”, thereby reducing the execution gap.
Gulf of Evaluation:
After completing the instrument survey or submitting a form, the system provides immediate feedback (such as success messages or result screens). This helps users clearly understand the outcome of their actions and reduces the evaluation gap.

🚀 Project Purpose

The purpose of this project is to provide affordable and accessible online music education for individuals who have limited budgets or do not have access to nearby music courses.

In addition, the platform also creates job opportunities for people who want to work as music instructors, allowing them to offer their lessons to a wider audience.

🏠 Home Page

The home page includes our main images and short information about our courses.
Users can quickly understand what our platform offers and how it can help them.

📊 Survey Page

On the survey page, we have a system that helps users find the most suitable musical instrument for their personality.
It also shows where they should start based on their level and interests.

🎸 Courses Page

On the courses page, users can see different musical instruments and course levels.

Beginner Level:
If the user has no experience, we teach basic finger movements and fundamental skills.
Intermediate Level:
If the user knows basic techniques but cannot play well, they practice simple songs to improve their skills.
Advanced Level:
If the user can play easy songs and wants to improve more, they receive professional training and practice more difficult pieces.

ℹ️ About Us Page

On the About Us page, detailed information is provided about our course, how it works, and where it is located.
Users can learn more about our system and our learning environment.
